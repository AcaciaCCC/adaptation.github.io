Definition of narratives

“Narratives follow a particular structure that describes the cause-and-effect relationships between events that take place over a particular time period that impact particular characters.” This “triumvirate of causality, temporality, and character represents a fairly standard definition of narrative communication”. (Dahlstrom 2014, 13614.)

Usefulness of narratives

“Empirical studies (…) suggest that narrative processing is generally more efficient. Narratives are often associated with increased recall, ease of comprehension, and shorter reading times.” (Dahlstrom 2014, 13615.)

“[N]arratives seem to offer intrinsic benefits in each of the four main steps of processing information: motivation and interest, allocating cognitive resources, elaboration, and transfer into long-term memory.” (Dahlstrom 2014, 13615.)

Source: Michael F. Dahlstrom: Using narratives and storytelling to communicate science with nonexpert audiences, in: PNAS, September 16, 2014, vol. 111, suppl. 4, www.pnas.org/cgi/doi/10.1073/pnas.1320645111 (accessed 13 September 2024), 13615.

Proposition for creating narratives for the CoCareSociety project

1.      A person is presented in their socio-economic living conditions and with their particular health condition.
2.     Actions are taken which, over time, influence the well-being of this person.
3.    These actions may have an effect on the social circumstances, living conditions, health, and/or economic situation of this person.

Age: 50 years

性別: 男
Gender: male

健康狀況: 健康狀況良好，但偶爾有心血管風險的初期症狀
Health conditions: Generally healthy, but occasionally shows early signs of cardiovascular risks

居住條件: 住在德國的城市公寓中，與配偶和兩個孩子一起生活
Living conditions: Lives in a city apartment in Germany with his spouse and two children

社會經濟背景: 中產階級，穩定的工作與收入，社交圈大，朋友和家人關係緊密
Socio-economic background: Middle-class, stable job and income, large social circle, close relationships with friends and family

照護視角: 暫無需要專門的照護，家庭成員會在必要時提供支持
Care perspectives: No special care required at the moment, family members provide support if needed

行為模式: 工作繁忙，常常久坐，缺乏運動，但有健康意識，偶爾參加體育活動
Behavioral patterns: Busy with work, often sedentary, lacks regular exercise but is health-conscious and occasionally participates in sports

潛在風險: 心血管疾病風險增加，壓力過大可能導致其他健康問題
Possible risks: Increased risk of cardiovascular disease, high stress could lead to other health issues

行動計畫: 鼓勵更多運動和減壓活動，定期健康檢查，及早發現潛在的健康問題。
Action plan: Encourage more exercise and stress-relief activities, regular health check-ups to detect potential issues early.

Action plan: Regular medical check-ups, diabetes management, physical therapy for arthritis, continue community involvement to reduce isolation.
